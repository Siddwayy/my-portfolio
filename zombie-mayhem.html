Zombie Mayhem: Wave-Based Survival Shooter

Zombie Mayhem is a first-person survival shooter developed solo in Unreal Engine 5 using Blueprints. Designed around a wave-based progression system, the game challenges players to survive against relentless hordes of zombies with escalating difficulty, reactive combat, and immersive feedback systems.

Core Gameplay

The game centers around surviving increasingly difficult waves of zombies in a post-apocalyptic floating island setting:

  • Wave System: A dynamic wave generator continuously ramps up challenge with larger groups and tougher enemies as rounds progress. Waves spawn from two separate locations, doubling zombies with each wave.
  • Combat Mechanics: Implemented responsive, trace-based shooting featuring recoil, precise hit detection, and immersive VFX for muzzle flashes and impacts. Different damage for headshots and body shots.
  • Player Health System: Includes damage overlays for visual feedback, health regeneration mechanics (5 HP every 5 seconds until 50 health), and a full fail state when the player is overwhelmed.

Artificial Intelligence

Fully autonomous enemy AI designed entirely in Blueprints:

  • Pawn Sensing: Zombies utilize Pawn Sensing to detect, chase, and engage players. They switch from slow walk to fast run when player is in acceptance radius.
  • Behavior States: Enemies transition between idle, chase, and attack states, ensuring varied encounters and reactive gameplay. Running animations and zombie sounds enhance immersion.
  • Attack System: Zombies attack the player with custom animations made through Blendspace, applying 20 HP damage in a loop until the zombie is killed.
  • Death Animations: Zombies fall to the ground and remain for 5 seconds before disappearing, adding to the game's realism.

  • Switch from slow walk to fast run when player is in acceptance radius
  • Enhances anticipation and immersion through clear audiovisual cues

Blood Hit Effects

  • Blood effect and hit animation play upon shooting a zombie
  • Provides clear and immediate feedback for damage output
  • Reinforces player actions through clear visual feedback

Dynamic Movement Animations

  • Different walk speeds and unique animations for idle and aiming states

  • Supports usability and perception principles by offering clear visual transitions

Shooting Feedback

  • Gun movement animation when firing

  • Bullet line trace through vector for accurate shooting mechanics

Health Regeneration & Damage Effects

  • Blood effects appear when player health is below 50

Game Story

The survival shooter is set in a post-apocalyptic Floating Island where the player must survive waves of zombies. The goal is to eliminate all zombies while managing health and ammunition, with increasing difficulty through a wave system.

Technical Implementation

Core Blueprint Systems

Key Blueprint References:
├─ BP_Zombie: Zombie AI, attack logic, death animations
├─ BP_FirstPersonCharacter: Player movement, shooting, health
├─ ZombieGameMode: Wave spawning and progression
├─ MainWidget_W: UI system with health bar and minimap
└─ WB_Minimap: Minimap functionality with player/zombie tracking

Blueprint Highlights:

  • Zombie AI: Custom event for health regeneration, pawn sensing for detection, blendspace animations for movement states
  • Combat System: Line trace shooting mechanics, surface-based hit effects, headshot/body shot damage differentiation
  • Wave System: Custom events for wave updates, timer-based spawning from multiple locations, difficulty scaling
  • UI System: Render opacity animations for smooth transitions, custom events for win/lose states, minimap position tracking

UX Testing Results

During UX testing, a non-classmate player was given the opportunity to play the game without prior instructions:

  • Positive Feedback: Player responded positively to enhanced zombie behavior, noting the sense of urgency created by running animations and zombie sounds
  • Blood Hit Effects: Well-received for providing immediate and satisfying feedback when shooting zombies
  • Dynamic Movement: Player adapted quickly to different movement states, particularly when switching between idle and aiming
  • Wave System: Increased engagement as difficulty escalated with each wave
  • Health System: Health regeneration and blood overlay helped player understand status without focusing solely on health bar
  • Minimap: Proved particularly useful for spatial awareness and strategic movement planning

Future Improvements Suggested: Distinct hitboxes for head and body shots to make aiming more strategic, and ammo restriction or reload mechanic to introduce resource management.
